NYPD Fare Evasion Enforcement 2 and its Disparate Impact

Project for Data Wrangling and Web Scraping at UC Santa Cruz, Fall 2019
Group Members: Leena, Woo, Dylan and Rebecca with Dhawal

On October 28, 2019, a video of around ten police officers tackling and pulling guns on a black teen for evading the $2.75 subway fare in New York City went viral. Communities were enraged to see this fierceley violent attack on a teen of color over such a small fare. A few months before, the MTA calculated that they were losing millions annually due to those who evade paying the transit fare. The MTA decided to deploy 500 new officers to watch for turnstile hoppers, in hopes of losing less money. While in theory this plan makes sense, we can't help but wonder. What kind of disparate impact against communities of color is happening? What about against lower income communities?

For each station, you'll see statistics aggregated at three different levels:

  • Community District: Refers to the "neighborhood" size zones within New York City. Official United States Census data is available at this level.
  • Transit District: Zones for the New York Police Department to oversee transit crime. A transit district will often be a few portions of neighboring transit lines. They are fairly larger than community districts. Fare evasion arrests and summonses are offered at this level.
  • Subway Station: Single subway station. A station may fall in multiple transit districts if the station has two or more lines with differing transit zones.
